Education

With over 7 years experience of tutoring elementary, middle, high school, and college students, both in-person and online, I'm complementing my double major with a minor in education with a focus on the secondary track of science and mathematics.

I currently work as an Undergraduate Learning Assistant (ULA) for the Department of Statistics and Applied Probability, in which I hold biweekly office hours, lead lab sections, and facilitate lectures alongside faculty to answer student questions. As a ULA for PSTAT 10: Principles of Data Science with R for over 4 quarters now, I’ve had the opportunity to add my own insights into the course based on what I’ve noticed students tend to need the most support. I’ve been able to shape and curate course content by constructing quizzes and leading review sessions.

In addition, I am a statistics tutor for Campus Learning Assistance Services, UCSB’s campus tutoring service. I lead drop-in hours to assist students in concepts such as distributions, random variables, Law of Large Numbers, Central Limit Theorems, and hypothesis testing.

As I strive to facilitate my students’ learning, my greatest priority as an instructor is to build rapport: I believe that a student is best able to learn in an environment where they feel comfortable enough to ask questions and make mistakes. My consistent efforts in building this rapport have successfully aided me in transforming the tutor-tutee relationship into friendships, ultimately encouraging students to attend drop-in hours more regularly and become more proactive with their education.

Projects

Diseases Of Affluence (Summer - Fall 2023)

I've most recently had the opportunity to take part in a research study examining how non-communicable diseases (e.g. hypertension, diabetes, or obesity) are associated with overnutrition and unhealthy eating habits, i.e. the Diseases of Affluence, particularly working with datasets in India. Working alongside Dr. Gurmeet Manku, Ph.D.; Dr. Uma Ravat, UCSB; and my collaborator Jake Bentley, we created survey data visualizations, fitted linear models, and performed nonparametric tests to isolate wealth and dietary factors having positive correlations with obesity and NCDs.

Lean On Me (Summer 2022)

Through my involvement with Lean On Me UCSB, I had the opportunity to join the data analytics team of Lean On Me National as a Data Visualization Intern in summer 2022. By analyzing over 6 years of existing temporal, binary, qualitative, and quantitative data, I created 100+ visualizations in R (particularly with ggplot2, Plotly) to display on the national and individual chapter websites. With this data, I drew bar graphs, time series charts, and box-and-whisker plots to effectively communicate the organization's impact. These visualizations have been used to supplement the organization in its marketing/funding pursuits and offered insight into how to update supporter training protocols based on user activity trends.
